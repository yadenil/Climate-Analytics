--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. DATA SOURCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Provider       : NASA Prediction of Worldwide Energy Resources (NASA POWER)
Model          : MERRA-2 (Modern-Era Retrospective Analysis for Research and
                 Applications, Version 2)
Type           : Satellite-derived reanalysis data
Resolution     : Daily temporal resolution, 0.5° × 0.625° spatial grid
Access         : https://power.larc.nasa.gov/data-access-viewer/

NASA POWER is a publicly available global database maintained by NASA's
Langley Research Center. It combines satellite observations with atmospheric
modeling to produce continuous, analysis-ready climate and weather estimates
for any location on Earth. MERRA-2 data are widely used in climate research,
agricultural modeling, and environmental policy work.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. DATASET COVERAGE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Countries      : Ethiopia, Kenya, Sudan, Tanzania, Nigeria
Period         : 01 January 2015 – 31 March 2026
Frequency      : Daily observations (one row per day per country)
Total rows     : ~4,100 rows per country (varies slightly by year)

Each country's data was extracted for a single representative point location
(the country's capital city coordinates), not averaged across the full country.
This means the data reflects local conditions at that point rather than a
national average.

Representative coordinates used:

  Country      Latitude    Longitude   Location
  ----------   ---------   ---------   -------------------
  Ethiopia      9.0300°N   38.7400°E   Addis Ababa
  Kenya         1.2921°S   36.8219°E   Nairobi
  Sudan        15.5007°N   32.5599°E   Khartoum
  Tanzania      6.7924°S   39.2083°E   Dar es Salaam
  Nigeria       6.5244°N    3.3792°E   Lagos

3. PARAMETER DEFINITIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Column          Unit        Description
-----------     --------    ----------------------------------------------------
YEAR            —           Calendar year of the observation.

DOY             —           Day of Year. An integer from 1 to 365 (or 366 in
                            leap years) representing the sequential day within
                            the year. Day 1 = January 1, Day 365 = December 31.
                            Must be combined with YEAR to reconstruct a full
                            date: e.g., YEAR=2015, DOY=32 → February 1, 2015.

T2M             °C          Mean daily air temperature measured at 2 meters
                            above the ground surface. This is the standard
                            reference height for surface temperature in
                            meteorology.

T2M_MAX         °C          Maximum air temperature at 2 meters recorded
                            during the day. Useful for identifying heat stress
                            events and extreme temperature days.

T2M_MIN         °C          Minimum air temperature at 2 meters recorded
                            during the day. Useful for identifying cold spells
                            and diurnal temperature patterns.

T2M_RANGE       °C          Daily temperature range, calculated as
                            T2M_MAX − T2M_MIN. A large range indicates a
                            continental or arid climate; a small range suggests
                            a maritime or humid climate.

PRECTOTCORR     mm/day      Bias-corrected total precipitation for the day,
                            measured in millimeters of rainfall per day.
                            "Corrected" means the raw satellite estimate has
                            been adjusted using ground-based observations to
                            improve accuracy. A value of 0.0 means no
                            measurable precipitation occurred that day.

RH2M            %           Relative humidity at 2 meters above the surface,
                            expressed as a percentage. Represents how much
                            moisture the air holds relative to its maximum
                            capacity at that temperature. Values near 100%
                            indicate saturated air (fog, rain). Values below
                            30% indicate dry conditions.

WS2M            m/s         Mean daily wind speed at 2 meters above the
                            surface, in meters per second. Measured at 2m
                            rather than the standard 10m to reflect near-
                            surface conditions relevant to agriculture and
                            human activity.

WS2M_MAX        m/s         Maximum wind speed at 2 meters recorded during
                            the day. Useful for identifying storm events and
                            wind stress conditions.

PS              kPa         Atmospheric surface pressure in kilopascals.
                            Pressure varies with altitude — locations at higher
                            elevation (e.g., Addis Ababa at ~2,355m) will show
                            consistently lower pressure than sea-level cities
                            (e.g., Lagos). Rapid pressure drops can indicate
                            incoming storm systems.

QV2M            g/kg        Specific humidity at 2 meters — the mass of water
                            vapor per kilogram of moist air, expressed in
                            grams per kilogram. Unlike relative humidity, this
                            is an absolute measure of moisture content and does
                            not depend on temperature.

4. MISSING VALUE CONVENTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

In the original NASA POWER source files, the value -999 is used as a sentinel
to indicate missing, unavailable, or out-of-range measurements. This occurs
when the satellite model cannot produce a reliable estimate for a given
parameter on a given day.

The CSV files provided for this challenge have been pre-processed. However,
you should still check for and handle -999 values during your data profiling
step, as they may be present in some country files.

When encountered, -999 should be replaced with NaN (Not a Number) before
performing any statistical analysis, as including -999 as a numeric value
will severely distort summary statistics, correlations, and plots.

  Recommended handling:
    import numpy as np
    df.replace(-999, np.nan, inplace=True)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. DATA QUALITY NOTES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The datasets provided for this challenge are real climate measurements and
reflect the complexity of working with real-world data. As part of this
challenge, you are expected to:

  - Identify and handle missing values (NaN and sentinel values)
  - Detect and investigate statistical outliers
  - Check for and resolve duplicate rows
  - Make and document informed decisions about how to clean the data

Not all data quality issues are immediately obvious. Careful profiling —
using df.describe(), df.isna(), df.duplicated(), and Z-score analysis —
is the recommended approach for systematic discovery.

There is no single correct way to handle every data quality issue. What
matters is that your decisions are clearly documented, justified in a
markdown cell, and consistent with the business objective.
